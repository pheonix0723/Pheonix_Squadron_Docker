Diabetic retinopathy (also known as diabetic eye disease), is a medical condition in which damage occurs to the retina due to diabetes mellitus.

Diabetic retinopathy affects up to 80 percent of those who have had both type 1 and type 2 diabetes for 20 years or more.

The longer a person has diabetes, the higher his or her chances of developing diabetic retinopathy.

Diabetic Retinopathy Information


Stage 01 : Mild

This is the earliest stage of diabetic retinopathy, characterized by tiny swellings in the blood vessels of the retina.

Stage 02 : Moderate

The tiny blood vessels further swell up, blocking blood flow to the retina and preventing proper nourishment.

Stage 03 : Severe

A larger section of blood vessels in the retina becomes blocked, causing a significant decrease in blood flow.

Stage 04 : Proliferative

At this advanced stage of the disease, new blood vessels continue to grow which are thin and weak and prone to bleeding.
